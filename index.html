About the Periodic Table

The periodic table of elements is a tabular arrangement of the chemical elements, ordered by their atomic number, electron configuration, and recurring chemical properties. It is widely used in chemistry, physics, and other sciences, and is generally seen as an icon of chemistry.

The table has been refined several times since Russian chemist Dmitri Mendeleev published his periodic table in 1869 based on the atomic mass of the elements. The modern table is based on the quantum mechanical model of electron configuration, which explains how electrons are structured within atoms.
